## 1. Insulin Protocol — The Sarcev Framework

The swarm's highest-density output. Milos Sarcev's approach was the dominant thread across all 10 experiments.

### The Core Insight: Nutrient Shuttle

Insulin's value is not glucose disposal — it is **nutrient shuttling during the hyperemia window**. During intense training, 60-70% of total blood volume (from a resting baseline of ~10%) accumulates in the working muscle group. Pre-workout insulin administration exploits this window: insulin directs amino acids, glucose, and creatine into muscle cells precisely when blood flow to those cells is maximal.

This is Sarcev's central departure from older protocols that used insulin only post-workout. Pre-workout administration captures the training hyperemia; post-workout misses it.

### Pharmacokinetics (Humalog)

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Onset | 15 minutes |
| Peak action | 45-75 minutes |
| Diminishing effects | After 2 hours |
| Complete clearance | 4 hours |

Humalog is preferred over longer-acting insulins because its defined action window aligns with a typical training session (60-90 minutes). Longer-acting insulins create unpredictable hypoglycemia risk hours after training.

### Dosing Protocol

- **Starting dose:** 5 IU Humalog for first-time users to assess individual response
- **Working dose:** 10 IU pre-workout (most common)
- **Advanced dose:** 15 IU pre-workout for experienced users
- **Timing:** Inject 45 minutes before training begins (some sources say 30 minutes; Sarcev specifically uses 45 to align peak insulin activity with peak training intensity)
- **Cardinal rule:** Minimum 10g fast-acting carbohydrates per 1 IU of insulin during the active window
- **Safety:** Glucometer monitoring before injection, during training, and post-workout. Fast-acting carbs (glucose tabs, juice) must be immediately available at all times.

### The Insulin Window — What to Eat and When

**During insulin active window (intra- and immediately post-workout):**
- Simple/fast-acting carbohydrates ONLY (dextrose, maltodextrin)
- **Zero added fat** — fat during the insulin window gets stored, not partitioned to muscle
- Protein from whey (fast-absorbing)

**Outside insulin window (all other meals):**
- Complex carbohydrates only (brown rice, sweet potatoes, oatmeal)
- Fat is permitted
- Protein from whole food sources (chicken, turkey, fish, lean beef)

This carbohydrate cycling principle — simple carbs only during insulin windows, complex carbs exclusively at other times — appeared in 8 of 10 experiments and is presented as the mechanism for using insulin without gaining fat.

### Intra-Workout Shake Composition

Sarcev's specific formulation (consistently reported across experiments):

| Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Water | 1 liter |
| Simple carbohydrates (dextrose/maltodextrin) | 50-60g |
| Whey protein | 20g |
| BCAAs | 6-20g |
| EAAs | 10-20g |
| Glutamine | 10g |
| Creatine | 3-10g |

Sipped continuously throughout training — not consumed all at once. The purpose is to maintain high circulating amino acid levels and prevent blood glucose from dropping while insulin is active.

### Post-Workout Protocol

1. **Immediately post-workout:** Shake with 50g whey isolate + 50g fast-digesting carbohydrates
2. **30-60 minutes later:** Solid meal of lean protein + complex carbohydrates (insulin effects are tapering, so transition to complex carbs)

### Who Should NOT Use This Protocol

Sarcev's own warning, found in every experiment: insulin is an **advanced protocol** that requires:
- Established training discipline (years of consistent training)
- Established nutrition discipline (precise macro tracking)
- Concurrent use of other PEDs (insulin is not a standalone compound)
- Understanding of hypoglycemia symptoms and emergency response
- Regular bloodwork monitoring

## 2. Growth Hormone — The Diabetogenic Problem

### GH Requires Insulin

Growth hormone is **diabetogenic** — it elevates fasting blood glucose and can cause insulin resistance. This was one of the swarm's most consistently identified cross-domain findings (appeared in 7 of 10 experiments):

> "GH raises blood glucose levels, which can lead to hyperglycemia if not managed with insulin."

For anyone using GH, exogenous insulin is not optional — it's pharmacologically necessary to prevent:
- Chronic hyperglycemia
- Pancreatic overload (the pancreas compensates by producing more endogenous insulin, leading to beta-cell fatigue)
- Progressive insulin resistance

### GH Dosing Protocol

- **Standard:** 2 IU with pre-workout insulin injection + 2 IU before bed
- **Advanced:** Up to 4-12 IU/day (split doses)
- **Mechanism:** GH promotes lipolysis and muscle growth via IGF-1 stimulation. Pre-workout timing synergizes with insulin's nutrient shuttle effect. Pre-bed timing supports recovery during sleep.

### The Pancreatic Overload Warning

Sarcev warns that excessive carbohydrate consumption without insulin control — especially when using GH — leads to pancreatic overload and accelerated insulin resistance. This finding appeared specifically in the serendipity-enabled experiments, suggesting it's a cross-domain connection (endocrinology x practical bodybuilding) that single-angle workers miss.

## 3. Compound Stacking — The Full Protocol

### Dosage Ranges (Aggregated Across Experiments)

| Compound | Conservative | Intermediate | Advanced |
|---|---|---|---|
| Testosterone Enanthate | 250 mg/week | 500 mg/week | 750-1000 mg/week |
| Trenbolone Acetate | — | 200-300 mg/week | 450-600 mg/week |
| Insulin (Humalog) | — | 5-10 IU pre-workout | 15 IU pre-workout |
| Growth Hormone | 2 IU/day | 4 IU/day (2+2) | 4-12 IU/day |
| Turinabol | 20 mg/day | 30-40 mg/day | 40-60 mg/day |
| Boldenone Undecylenate | — | 400 mg/week | 600-800 mg/week |
| Actovegin | — | — | 10-20 mL/day IM |
| LGD-4033 (Ligandrol) | — | 10 mg/day | 10 mg/day |

### Administration Notes

- **Testosterone Enanthate:** Split into 2 injections per week (half-life 4.5-8 days) to maintain stable serum levels
- **Trenbolone Acetate:** Every other day or 3x/week (short half-life 2-3 days requires frequent dosing)
- **Boldenone Undecylenate:** 2 injections per week, stimulates appetite and promotes lean mass
- **Turinabol:** Oral, daily. Enhances strength without significant water retention. Split doses for stable blood levels.
- **LGD-4033:** Oral, daily (morning). SARM that preserves lean tissue during cutting phases with fewer androgenic side effects.
- **Actovegin:** IM post-workout. Enhances oxygen and nutrient delivery to tissues. Cycled with other compounds.

### Phase Structure

Every experiment produced a phased protocol, consistently following this pattern:

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4): Foundation**
- Testosterone only (or + Turinabol + low-dose GH)
- Establish nutritional discipline and training baseline
- Introduce micronutrient support

**Phase 2 (Weeks 5-8): Expansion**
- Add Trenbolone + Insulin + increased GH
- Introduce intra-workout nutrition protocol
- Begin carbohydrate cycling

**Phase 3 (Weeks 9-12): Peak**
- Add remaining compounds (Boldenone, Actovegin, LGD-4033)
- Maximum dosages
- Full insulin/GH/nutrition protocol operational

Transition between phases is based on: bloodwork stability, body composition changes, strength progression, and absence of adverse effects.

## 5. Daily Nutrition Framework

### Macronutrient Targets

| Macro | Daily Target | Distribution |
|---|---|---|
| Protein | 300-350g | 5-6 meals, whole food sources |
| Carbohydrates | 400-500g | Cycled between high/low days, type varies by insulin timing |
| Fat | 70-90g | Excluded during insulin active window |

### Carbohydrate Cycling

- **High-carb days (training days):** Full macro targets, simple carbs during insulin windows, complex carbs otherwise
- **Low-carb days (rest days):** Reduced carbohydrates, no exogenous insulin
- **Justin Harris protocol variant:** 4-10 IU Humalog at three meals on high-carb days only, titrating dose to avoid hypoglycemia. Insulin is NOT used on low-carb days.

### Protein Sources (Sarcev-recommended)
Chicken, turkey, fish, lean beef — whole food sources at every meal except intra/post-workout where whey is used for speed of absorption.

### Carbohydrate Sources
- **Insulin window:** Dextrose, maltodextrin (fast-acting only)
- **Other meals:** Brown rice, sweet potatoes, oatmeal (complex only)

| Marker | Purpose | Frequency |
|---|---|---|

| Estradiol | Aromatization monitoring | Every 4-6 weeks |
| IGF-1 | GH effectiveness | Every 4-6 weeks |
| Fasting Glucose | Insulin sensitivity, GH-induced hyperglycemia | Weekly when using insulin/GH |
| Liver Enzymes (ALT, AST) | Hepatotoxicity from orals (Turinabol) | Every 4-6 weeks |
| Lipid Profile | Cardiovascular risk (Trenbolone is particularly harsh) | Every 4-6 weeks |
| Hematocrit & Hemoglobin | Erythrocytosis risk from androgens | Every 4-6 weeks |
| Kidney Function | Overall organ health | Every 4-6 weeks |
| Complete Blood Count (CBC) | Blood viscosity monitoring | Every 4-6 weeks |

### Blood Glucose Monitoring Protocol (Insulin Users)

- Before insulin injection
- 30 minutes into training
- Immediately post-workout
- 2 hours post-workout (confirming insulin clearance)

### Red Flags Requiring Immediate Intervention

- Fasting glucose consistently elevated → GH-induced insulin resistance developing
- Hypoglycemia symptoms during training → reduce insulin dose, increase intra-workout carbs
- Liver enzymes >3x upper normal → discontinue oral compounds
- Hematocrit >54% → blood donation or dose reduction

## 7. Cross-Domain Connections (Serendipity Findings)

These findings were produced specifically by the serendipity wave — a separate worker pass that looks for connections between angles that individual workers missed.

### Blood Volume Redistribution + Nutrient Timing

> "During intense workouts, up to 60-70% of total blood volume accumulates in the working muscle group, making this an optimal time for nutrient delivery."

At rest, roughly 10% of blood volume is in muscle tissue. During intense bodybuilding-style training (giant sets, supersets, forced reps), this rises to 60-70%. This 6-7x increase in blood flow is the physiological basis for the entire nutrient shuttle concept — compounds (insulin, amino acids, glucose, creatine) delivered during this window reach muscle cells at dramatically higher concentrations than at any other time.

### GH-Insulin Endocrine Interaction

GH and insulin have opposing effects on blood glucose but synergistic effects on muscle growth. The resolution:
- GH promotes lipolysis (fat burning) and elevates blood glucose
- Insulin promotes glucose uptake and protein synthesis
- **Timing them together** means: GH mobilizes fatty acids for energy while insulin shuttles amino acids and glucose into muscle. The diabetogenic effect of GH is managed by the exogenous insulin.

This is not simply "take both" — it requires precise timing. GH before bed (when insulin is NOT active) supports overnight lipolysis and recovery. GH with pre-workout insulin supports the anabolic window. The two timing slots serve different physiological purposes.

### Pancreatic Overload Risk

The combination of high carbohydrate intake + GH (diabetogenic) + no exogenous insulin creates a dangerous metabolic trap:
1. GH raises blood glucose
2. High carb intake raises blood glucose further
3. The pancreas compensates by producing more endogenous insulin
4. Over weeks/months, beta-cell fatigue develops
5. Insulin resistance progresses, eventually requiring exogenous insulin just to maintain normal glucose levels

This is why Sarcev insists on exogenous insulin from the beginning of GH use — not to add an anabolic effect, but to prevent the pancreas from being destroyed by the metabolic demands of the protocol.

### Integration Across Domains

> "Integration of compounds across different domains — anabolic steroids, insulin, and growth hormone — demonstrates the importance of a synergistic approach."

The swarm's most complete protocol (experiment 10, 96K corpus) showed that no single compound class works in isolation. The pharmacokinetic reasoning requires understanding how testosterone (provides anabolic base), trenbolone (nitrogen retention), insulin (nutrient partitioning), GH (lipolysis + IGF-1), and micronutrients (insulin sensitivity) interact as a system. Removing any one element degrades the others' effectiveness or creates safety risks.

## 8. What the Swarm Missed

Given that this was a closed-corpus analysis (no external data, no web search), several topics were absent from the findings:

1. **PCT (Post-Cycle Therapy)** — No experiment produced findings about recovery after the cycle ends
2. **Psychological effects** — Trenbolone's documented mental side effects (insomnia, aggression) were not explored
3. **Long-term health outcomes** — All findings are about protocol optimization, none about long-term organ damage risks
4. **Individual variation** — Findings assume a generic "advanced bodybuilder" with no discussion of genetic polymorphisms affecting drug metabolism
5. **Legal/procurement** — No findings about sourcing, legality, or quality control of compounds
6. **Female-specific protocols** — Entire corpus and all findings assume male physiology

These gaps reflect the corpus, not the architecture. Workers with external search tools would likely discover these dimensions.
