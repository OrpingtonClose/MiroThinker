### 1. Humalog (Insulin Lispro) Timing Protocols

**Finding 1.1: Pre-Workout Timing**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Humalog administered 15–20 minutes prior to training. Dosage is typically in the range of 2–6 IU for initiation.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Insulin facilitates glucose uptake into skeletal muscle cells. Pre-workout administration is intended to increase intramuscular glycogen availability and amino acid uptake during the training session, theoretically enhancing performance and reducing catabolism.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal (derived from user reports within the bodybuilding community).
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Synergizes with the post-workout carbohydrate load; interaction with GH supplementation (see Finding 4.1).

**Finding 1.2: Intra-Workout Timing**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Humalog administered mid-session, typically 30–45 minutes into training, at a low dose of 1–3 IU mixed with a rapidly digestible carbohydrate source (e.g., dextrose in water).
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** To blunt catabolism during the workout by providing a direct fuel source to muscles and preventing cortisol spikes. The goal is to maintain an anabolic state without causing acute hypoglycemia.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Directly linked to glucose monitoring frequency (Finding 3.1); often paired with GH administration (Finding 4.1).

**Finding 1.3: Post-Workout Timing**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Humalog administered immediately upon completion of training, in conjunction with a high-glycemic carbohydrate meal (e.g., maltodextrin or simple sugars). The insulin-to-carb ratio is a key variable.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** To capitalize on the "insulin sensitivity window" post-exercise, driving glucose and amino acids (e.g., from hydrolyzed whey protein) into muscle cells to replenish glycogen stores and initiate protein synthesis.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Heavily dependent on Carbohydrate Management (Finding 7.1); foundational for the "shuttle" effect involving GH (Finding 4.1).

### 2. Dosage Escalation

**Finding 2.1: Starting Doses**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Beginners are advised to start with a single daily dose of 2 IU, typically administered subcutaneously in the abdominal area 15–20 minutes before the largest meal of the day or training session.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** To assess individual sensitivity and tolerance to exogenous insulin before increasing the risk of severe hypoglycemia.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Baseline for escalation; requires concurrent glucose monitoring (Finding 3.1).

**Finding 2.2: Working Doses**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Experienced users may utilize 4–8 IU per administration, divided into pre-, intra-, and post-workout protocols. The intra-workout dose is usually the smallest fraction of the total daily dose.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** To maintain a basal level of insulin sensitivity while providing acute anabolic pulses around training.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Requires strict carbohydrate management (Finding 7.1) to match the insulin load.

**Finding 2.3: Maximum Documented Doses**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** The corpus mentions documented upper limits of 10–12 IU per administration for advanced users. Total daily doses exceeding 20 IU are noted as entering high-risk territory with diminishing returns.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** At these doses, the risk of profound hypoglycemia outweighs any potential anabolic benefit. The body may downregulate insulin receptors as a protective mechanism.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal (with warnings of severe risk).
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Directly necessitates aggressive glucose monitoring (Finding 3.1) and glucagon preparedness (Finding 9.1).

### 3. Glucose Monitoring

**Finding 3.1: Thresholds and Timing**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Blood glucose is monitored via glucometer before meals and immediately pre- and post-workout. Critical thresholds are defined as < 70 mg/dL (hypoglycemia alert) and > 180 mg/dL (hyperglycemia).
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** To maintain euglycemia (normal blood sugar) and ensure that administered insulin has sufficient substrate (glucose) to act upon, preventing dangerous drops in blood sugar.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Informs dosing escalation (Finding 2.2) and emergency protocols (Finding 3.2).

**Finding 3.2: Emergency Protocols**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** If hypoglycemia (< 70 mg/dL) is detected, immediate ingestion of 15–20 grams of fast-acting carbohydrates (e.g., glucose tablets, juice) is mandated. If unconscious, glucagon is administered.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** To rapidly reverse hypoglycemia by providing an exogenous source of blood glucose.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal (based on emergency medical standards).
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Directly linked to Safety Protocols (Finding 9.1); reliance on Glucagon (Finding 9.1).

### 4. GH (Growth Hormone) Synergy

**Finding 4.1: Timing Relative to Insulin**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** GH is typically administered 30–60 minutes after a Humalog injection or 2–3 hours before a meal containing insulin. The "shuttle" method involves insulin driving glucose into cells, creating a low-glucose state that theoretically stimulates GH release.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Insulin-induced hypoglycemia is a potent stimulator of GH secretion. The synergy aims to amplify the anabolic and lipolytic effects of both hormones.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Inextricably links Insulin Protocols (Finding 1) with GH dosing (Finding 4.2).

**Finding 4.2: Dosage Ratios**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Common ratios mentioned are 1 IU of Humalog to 2–4 IU of GH, administered in a staggered fashion to avoid stacking effects that could cause severe hypoglycemia.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** To balance the catabolic effect of insulin with the lipolytic effect of GH, ensuring fat loss while preserving muscle mass.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Defines the interaction between Finding 4.1 and the dosing escalation in Finding 2.

### 5. IGF-1 Interactions

**Finding 5.1: LR3 IGF-1 Timing**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Long R3 IGF-1 (mechanIGF-1) is often dosed at 20–50 mcg 30 minutes post-workout or in the morning, independent of insulin spikes.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** LR3 IGF-1 has a long half-life and binds to IGF-1 receptors, promoting muscle cell hypertrophy and nutrient partitioning. It is used to extend the anabolic signal beyond the insulin window.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** May be used in conjunction with insulin but is often run in cycles to avoid receptor downregulation.

**Finding 5.2: DES IGF-1 (Methionine IGF-1)**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** DES IGF-1 is typically administered via intramuscular injection at 20–40 mcg, split into 2–3 daily doses, often away from high-insulin periods.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** DES (Des(1-3) IGF-1) binds primarily to the insulin receptor, exhibiting strong hypoglycemic effects. Its use is to stimulate localized growth factors in muscle tissue.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** High risk of severe hypoglycemia when combined with Humalog (Finding 1); requires extreme caution with glucose monitoring (Finding 3).

### 6. Metformin as Insulin Sensitizer

**Finding 6.1: Dosing and Timing**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Metformin is taken at a dose of 500–1000 mg, 1–2 times daily with meals. It is often taken in the morning and early afternoon, avoiding administration close to intense training or high-dose insulin windows.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** To improve peripheral insulin sensitivity, allowing the body to use less exogenous insulin to achieve the same glucose-lowering effect, thereby reducing the required dosage.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal (off-label use).
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Reduces the required dose of Humalog (Finding 2); may mitigate some of the hypoglycemic risk.

### 7. Carbohydrate Management

**Finding 7.1: Grams per IU**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** A common starting ratio is 10–15 grams of carbohydrates per 1 IU of Humalog. Intra-workout may use a 2:1 or 3:1 carb-to-insulin ratio.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** To ensure that the administered insulin has sufficient substrate to prevent hypoglycemia while still driving glucose into the muscle cells.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Directly dictates the success of the insulin protocol; high-GI carbs are preferred for rapid uptake (Finding 7.2).

**Finding 7.2: Glycemic Index Considerations**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** High-GI carbohydrates (e.g., dextrose, maltodextrin) are preferred for post-workout and intra-workout feeds to ensure a rapid spike in blood glucose and insulin absorption. Lower-GI carbs are reserved for non-training meals.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** High-GI carbs cause a rapid influx of glucose into the bloodstream, matching the rapid onset of Humalog and preventing a lag that could cause hypoglycemia.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Integral to the Carbohydrate Management strategy (Finding 7.1); affects the speed of insulin action.

### 8. Anabolic Compound Stacking

**Finding 8.1: Testosterone Base**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Testosterone enanthate or cypionate is used as a base at doses of 200–600 mg per week. Insulin is added to this foundation to enhance nutrient partitioning.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Testosterone provides a solid anabolic foundation, while insulin increases the uptake of nutrients into the muscle cells created by the testosterone, amplifying muscle growth.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** The primary anabolic agent that insulin seeks to potentiate; requires careful management to avoid estrogenic side effects.

**Finding 8.2: Trenbolone Interactions with Glucose**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Trenbolone acetate or enanthate (50–100 mg every other day) is noted to significantly increase insulin resistance. Users must drastically increase carbohydrate intake to compensate.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Trenbolone is a progestin that induces severe insulin resistance, meaning higher doses of insulin (and therefore carbohydrates) are required to achieve the same glucose-lowering effect.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Creates a challenging scenario for insulin protocols (Finding 2), requiring aggressive carbohydrate management (Finding 7) to prevent hypoglycemia.

### 9. Safety Protocols

**Finding 9.1: Glucagon Kit and Glucose Tablets**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** A glucagon emergency kit (1 mg) and rapid-acting glucose tablets (15g) must be on-hand at all times. The user must be trained on glucagon administration.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Glucagon raises blood sugar by stimulating glycogenolysis and gluconeogenesis in the liver, providing a backup if oral glucose cannot be consumed during severe hypoglycemia.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal (medical standard of care).
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** The failsafe for the entire insulin protocol (Findings 2, 3); non-negotiable for safety.

**Finding 9.2: Monitoring Frequency**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** During active insulin use, blood glucose is checked a minimum of 4–6 times per day (pre-meals, pre-bed, nocturnal if possible).
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** To detect trends and prevent asymptomatic hypoglycemia, especially during the night when it can be fatal.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Informs all dosing decisions (Findings 2, 3); dictates the strictness of the protocol.

### 10. Periodization

**Finding 10.1: On-Season vs. Off-Season**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Insulin is primarily utilized during "on-season" (bulking phases) to maximize nutrient shuttling and muscle growth. It is generally avoided during "off-season" (cutting phases) or used very minimally to prevent insulin resistance.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** During a caloric surplus, the body is more tolerant of the exogenous insulin load. During a caloric deficit, the risk of hypoglycemia increases, and the body's natural metabolic flexibility is preferred.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Dictates the intensity and frequency of the protocol (Findings 2, 3).

**Finding 10.2: Deload Protocols**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Every 4–6 weeks, a "deload" week is recommended where insulin dosage is reduced by 50% or eliminated entirely. Carbohydrate intake is also lowered proportionally.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** To prevent receptor downregulation and insulin resistance, maintaining the body's responsiveness to the hormone.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Critical for the sustainability of long-term insulin use (Finding 2); prevents the need for higher, dangerous doses (Finding 2.3).
