### Humalog (Insulin Lispro) Timing & Dosage Protocols

**1. Pre-Workout Humalog Administration (The Hyperemia Principle)**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Insulin lispro (Humalog); 5–10 IU (starting/conservative), 15–30 IU (advanced/maximum); administered subcutaneously 15–30 minutes prior to resistance training.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Activation of the PI3K/Akt pathway leading to GLUT4 translocation to the sarcolemma. Sarcev’s core methodology relies on exercise-induced hyperemia (a 15-to-20-fold increase in localized blood flow to working muscles). Pre-workout insulin ensures peak serum insulin coincides with peak localized blood flow, driving nutrients preferentially into skeletal muscle rather than adipose tissue.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal / Extrapolated clinical (hemodynamics).
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Nitric oxide (NO) pharmacology; PDE5 inhibitors (e.g., tadalafil) used in pulmonary hypertension and bodybuilding to further amplify the hyperemic effect.

**2. Post-Workout Insulin Administration**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Insulin lispro (Humalog) or regular human insulin (Humulin R); 5–15 IU; administered immediately post-workout.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Rapid replenishment of depleted muscular glycogen and potent suppression of exercise-induced cortisol. Insulin inhibits proteasome-mediated muscle breakdown and shifts the body from a catabolic to a highly anabolic state.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal / Extrapolated clinical.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Glucocorticoid receptor antagonism; Cushing's syndrome management (mitigating hypercortisolemia).

### Carbohydrate Management & Intra-Workout Nutrition

**3. Intra-Workout Carbohydrate Titration**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Highly Branched Cyclic Dextrin (HBCD) or Vitargo; 7–10 grams of carbohydrates per 1 IU of insulin; sipped continuously from 10 minutes pre-workout until the end of the session.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Rapid gastric emptying and intestinal absorption to match the pharmacokinetic peak of Humalog (which peaks at 60–90 minutes). This prevents severe hypoglycemia while maintaining a continuous gradient of glucose for GLUT4-mediated cellular uptake.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal / Case reports.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Endurance sports nutrition; continuous glucose infusion protocols in hyperinsulinemic-euglycemic clamp studies.

**4. Essential Amino Acid (EAA) Co-Administration**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Free-form EAAs (leucine-heavy); 15–30g; mixed with the intra-workout carbohydrate source.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Insulin regulates the System A and ASC amino acid transporters. High serum insulin forces the EAAs into the myocyte, creating a hyperaminoacidemic state that potently stimulates the mTORC1 pathway, initiating ribosomal protein synthesis.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Extrapolated clinical.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Sarcopenia and cachexia interventions in oncology and geriatrics.

### Safety Protocols & Glucose Monitoring

**5. Capillary Blood Glucose Monitoring**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Glucometer checks; Targets: Pre-workout (>100 mg/dL), Intra-workout (80–120 mg/dL); Timing: Pre-injection, 45 minutes intra-workout, immediately post-workout, and 2 hours post-workout.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Real-time monitoring to prevent neuroglycopenia (CNS energy deprivation). Due to the massive glucose sink created by hyperemic muscle + exogenous insulin, serum glucose can drop precipitously despite carbohydrate ingestion.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal (bodybuilding standard) / Clinical (endocrinology).
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus (T1DM) intensive glycemic management.

**6. Hypoglycemia Emergency Protocol**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Dextrose tablets (15–30g oral) or Glucagon kit (1mg intramuscular); administered immediately if blood glucose drops below 60 mg/dL or if symptomatic (diaphoresis, tremors, confusion).
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Dextrose provides immediate mucosal/gastric glucose absorption. Glucagon binds to hepatic glucagon receptors, stimulating glycogenolysis and gluconeogenesis to rapidly rescue serum glucose levels.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Clinical study / Standard medical protocol.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Emergency medicine; EMS protocols for diabetic coma.

### Growth Hormone (GH) & IGF-1 Synergy

**7. Somatropin (GH) and Insulin Synergy**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Recombinant Human Growth Hormone (rhGH); 2–10 IU; administered subcutaneously 15–30 minutes pre-workout, concurrently with insulin.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** GH is diabetogenic and lipolytic, inducing transient insulin resistance by interfering with PI3K signaling. Exogenous insulin overrides this resistance. Together, they create a synergistic anabolic environment: GH mobilizes fatty acids (sparing glucose) while insulin forces glucose/amino acids into muscle, and both massively upregulate hepatic and localized IGF-1 production.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal / Extrapolated clinical.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Anti-aging medicine; HIV-lipodystrophy treatments.

**8. IGF-1 DES (Truncated) Administration**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** IGF-1 DES (1-3); 20–50 mcg; administered via bilateral intramuscular injection into the trained muscle group immediately pre-workout.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** The DES variant lacks the first three amino acids at the N-terminus, preventing it from binding to Insulin-like Growth Factor-Binding Proteins (IGFBPs). This allows for immediate, unbound receptor interaction in the localized, hyperemic tissue, promoting satellite cell activation and hyperplasia.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Experimental tissue regeneration; burn victim recovery research.

**9. IGF-1 LR3 Administration**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** IGF-1 LR3; 40–50 mcg; administered subcutaneously post-workout or on non-training days.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** The Arg3 substitution and 13-amino-acid extension increase the half-life to 20–30 hours. It provides a systemic, sustained activation of the IGF-1 receptor, complementing the acute spikes of the pre-workout Humalog/GH/DES protocol.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Pediatric endocrinology (treatment of Laron syndrome / primary IGF-1 deficiency).

### Anabolic Compound Stacking Interactions

**10. Testosterone Base Synergism**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Testosterone (Enanthate/Cypionate); 500–1000+ mg/week; administered via intramuscular injection 1-2 times weekly.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Testosterone diffuses into the myocyte, binds to the androgen receptor (AR), and translocates to the nucleus to increase gene transcription (creating new mRNA for muscle proteins). Insulin acts at the translational level (ribosomes). The combination maximizes both the "blueprint" (transcription) and the "construction" (translation) phases of protein synthesis.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Extrapolated clinical.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Hypogonadism; severe burn trauma pharmacology.

**11. Trenbolone and Glucose Partitioning**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Trenbolone (Acetate/Enanthate); 200–600 mg/week; administered via intramuscular injection.
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Trenbolone potently upregulates IGF-1 receptor density in skeletal muscle and drastically improves nutrient partitioning. It increases peripheral insulin sensitivity, which lowers the required dose of exogenous insulin to achieve a biological effect, simultaneously increasing the risk of unexpected hypoglycemia if insulin doses are not adjusted downward.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal / Veterinary science.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Bovine agriculture (feed efficiency and lean mass accretion).

### Metformin & Periodization

**12. Metformin as an Insulin Sensitizer**
*   **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Metformin hydrochloride; 500–1000 mg; administered orally with carbohydrate-containing meals (expressly kept away from the peri-workout insulin window).
*   **Physiological Mechanism:** Activates AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK) in the liver, suppressing hepatic gluconeogenesis. In skeletal muscle, it increases insulin-stimulated glucose uptake. Bodybuilders use it to maintain insulin receptor sensitivity during periods of high exogenous insulin and high-carbohydrate feeding.
*   **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal / Clinical.
*   **Cross-Domain Connections:** Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM); PCOS; longevity research.
