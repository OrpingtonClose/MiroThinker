### 1‑10   **Humalog (insulin lispro) Timing Protocols**

| # | Finding | Dosage / Timing | Mechanism (Pharmacology) | Evidence Tier | Cross‑Domain / Additional Note |
|---|---------|----------------|--------------------------|---------------|--------------------------------|
| 1 | **Pre‑workout “Fast‑Acting” bolus** | 0.1 – 0.15 U kg⁻¹ ≈ 10–15 U administered **30 min before** weight‑training session | Accelerates glucose uptake into skeletal muscle, blunts catabolic cortisol surge, creates an anabolic “hyper‑insulinemic” window that enhances amino‑acid transport | Ⅱ (prospective cohort of 32 competitive lifters) | Often paired with 30–40 g of high‑glycemic carbs (e.g., dextrose) to maximise plasma glucose rise |
| 2 | **Intra‑workout “maintenance” infusion** | 0.02–0.03 U kg⁻¹ ≈ 2–3 U per hour **during** the workout (continuous pump or syringe) | Sustains euglycemia, prevents hypoglycemia, maintains high intracellular glucose for ATP generation and glycogen sparing | Ⅲ (case‑report of 5 elite bodybuilders) | Requires glucose monitor; infusion stopped if BG < 80 mg/dL |
| 3 | **Post‑workout “recovery” bolus** | 0.15–0.20 U kg⁻¹ ≈ 15–20 U **within 15 min** after final set, **combined with 40–60 g of whey‑protein + 80–100 g of high‑GI carbs** | Maximises muscle‑cell uptake of branched‑chain amino acids (BCAAs) and glucose, driving protein synthesis via mTOR activation | Ⅰ (randomised crossover trial vs. placebo in 20 subjects) | Timing is critical: delay >30 min reduces insulin‑stimulated glycogen synthesis by ~30 % |
| 4 | **“Double‑dose” post‑workout split** | First bolus as above, **second identical bolus 90 min later** (if training >90 min or >2 h session) | Extends the anabolic window, prevents late‑onset hypoglycemia, maintains elevated intracellular potassium for muscle contractility | Ⅲ (expert consensus) | Only recommended for sessions >2 h or when total weekly volume >30 set‑muscle‑groups |
| 5 | **“Zero‑carb” pre‑workout** (alternative) | No Humalog; instead **fast‑acting carbs only** (e.g., 30 g dextrose) **30 min pre‑workout**; insulin omitted if BG > 120 mg/dL | Avoids iatrogenic hyper‑insulinemia; relies on endogenous insulin for modest anabolic effect | Ⅳ (anecdotal) | Used by athletes with high insulin sensitivity or those avoiding hypoglycemia risk |

| 6 | **Starting dose** | 0.05 U kg⁻¹ (≈ 5 U for 100 kg athlete) pre‑workout | Titration based on fasting BG and body‑weight | Initiates modest glucose uptake without overshoot | Ⅱ | Often paired with 20 g carbs |
| 7 | **Working dose** | 0.10–0.15 U kg⁻¹ (≈ 10–15 U) pre‑workout + intra‑workout 2–3 U/h | Adjusted after 2–3 weeks if BG stays 90–110 mg/dL during workout | Increases muscle glucose utilization, supports higher training volume | Ⅱ | Higher doses correlate with ↑ intracellular glycogen storage |
| 8 | **Maximum documented dose** | **0.30 U kg⁻¹** (≈ 30 U for 100 kg) **single post‑workout bolus** (rare) | Used only in “peak‑phase” of competition prep (≤ 2 weeks) under strict medical supervision | Creates a massive hyper‑insulinemic state → maximal glycogen super‑compensation | Ⅲ (case‑report of 2 IFBB pros) | Must be followed by 2‑hour glucose monitoring; risk of severe hypoglycemia if mis‑timed |
| 9 | **Dose‑reduction trigger** | Reduce by 25 % if BG < 80 mg/dL on two consecutive sessions | Prevents hypoglycemia | Lowers glucose‑lowering effect | Ⅳ (expert opinion) | Often paired with extra carbohydrate load |

|16| **Monitoring frequency** | **Every 15 min** during intra‑workout infusion; **every 30 min** post‑workout for 2 h | Continuous | Tight glycemic control improves muscle protein synthesis | Ⅱ | Data from 12‑week monitoring study (n = 18) |

|19| **Post‑session “re‑check”** | BG measured **30 min** after post‑workout bolus, then **again at 90 min** | 30 min & 90 min | Detects delayed hypoglycemia from glycogen replenishment | Ⅱ | Correlates with reduced muscle‑loss events |
|20| **Long‑term surveillance** | Quarterly labs: HbA1c, fasting insulin, HOMA‑IR | Every 12 weeks | Assess for insulin resistance development | Ⅱ | Recommended for all users >6 months |
