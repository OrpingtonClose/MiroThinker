**ACADEMIC DISCLAIMER:** The following analysis extracts pharmacological parameters reported in bodybuilding literature, seminar transcripts, athlete case reports, and community corpus surrounding the Milos Sarcev methodology. Insulin, GH, IGF-1 variants, and anabolic-androgenic steroids (AAS) are prescription medications with narrow therapeutic indices. Their use for physique enhancement is off-label, lacks randomized controlled trial (RCT) validation, and carries significant morbidity/mortality risk (primarily severe hypoglycemia, insulin resistance, and cardiometabolic strain). All dosages and timings below are documented as *reported parameters* for academic pharmacology reference only and do not constitute clinical guidance.

---

### **1. Humalog (Insulin Lispro) Timing Protocols**

**Finding #1**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Insulin lispro (Humalog), 2–5 IU, administered 15–30 minutes pre-workout with 20–40g fast-acting carbohydrates.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Lispro's rapid onset (15 min) aligns with exercise-induced GLUT4 translocation. Pre-workout dosing aims to prime intracellular glucose and amino acid uptake before mechanical tension begins, theoretically augmenting glycogen availability and cell volumization.
- **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal/bodybuilding seminar corpus; mechanistic extrapolation from exercise physiology.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Synergizes with intra-workout carbohydrate-electrolyte solutions; catecholamine surge during training initially antagonizes insulin via β2-adrenergic signaling, potentially delaying hypoglycemia but creating a rebound drop post-exercise.

**Finding #2**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Insulin lispro, 1–2 IU, administered intra-workout mixed with 30–50g high-GI carbohydrate drink (rarely practiced).
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Attempts to sustain insulin-mediated nutrient partitioning during prolonged training. However, skeletal muscle contraction already maximizes non-insulin-dependent GLUT4 translocation, making exogenous insulin pharmacodynamically redundant and increasing hypoglycemia risk.
- **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal case reports; multiple documented episodes of intra-session neuroglycopenia.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Interacts with cortisol and epinephrine kinetics; high sympathetic tone blunts insulin receptor substrate-1 (IRS-1) phosphorylation, reducing efficacy while preserving hypoglycemic potential.

**Finding #3**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Insulin lispro, 5–10 IU, administered immediately post-workout with 50–100g high-GI carbohydrates and 30–50g hydrolyzed whey/isolate.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Post-exercise insulin sensitivity peaks due to AMPK activation, glycogen depletion, and elevated muscle blood flow. Lispro administration at this window maximizes glycogen synthase activation, mTORC1 signaling via PI3K/Akt, and suppresses FOXO-mediated proteolysis.
- **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal consensus; strongly supported by clinical exercise metabolism literature on post-exercise nutrient timing.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Potentiates creatine monohydrate uptake via sodium-glucose cotransporter interactions; leucine threshold (~3g) synergizes with insulin for maximal ribosomal biogenesis.

### **2. Dosage Escalation**

**Finding #4**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Starting dose: 2–5 IU per session, 1x daily post-workout.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Low-dose initiation assesses individual insulin sensitivity, hepatic glycogen status, and counterregulatory hormone responsiveness. Minimizes risk of receptor oversaturation and abrupt hypoglycemia.
- **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal protocol standard; aligns with clinical insulin titration principles in naïve patients.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Baseline insulin sensitivity heavily modulated by concurrent AAS, body fat percentage, training volume, and dietary carbohydrate load.

**Finding #5**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Working dose: 5–15 IU per session, 1–2x daily (typically post-workout and/or with largest meal).
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Sustained activation of IRS-1/PI3K/Akt pathway promotes protein synthesis, glycogen storage, and anti-catabolism. Chronic dosing induces adaptive downregulation of insulin receptors, necessitating dose or carbohydrate escalation.
- **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal/bodybuilding literature; mechanistic parallel to clinical insulin resistance development.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Tolerance development correlates with increased visceral adiposity, elevated fasting insulin, and blunted hypoglycemic awareness; often managed with carb cycling or insulin-sensitizer adjuncts.

**Finding #6**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Maximum documented doses: 20–50+ IU per session in advanced/elite circles.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** High-dose insulin saturates insulin receptors and begins cross-activating IGF-1 receptors due to structural homology. Promotes profound lipogenesis via SREBP-1c activation and increases risk of receptor internalization, beta-cell feedback suppression, and severe hypoglycemia.
- **Evidence Quality:** Case reports, anecdotal elite bodybuilding corpus, medical literature on insulin misuse.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Strongly associated with dyslipidemia, hepatic steatosis, and impaired glucose tolerance; often necessitates concurrent metformin, berberine, or strict periodization to mitigate metabolic strain.

### **3. Glucose Monitoring**

**Finding #7**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Glucose threshold maintenance: >70 mg/dL; intervention triggered at <60 mg/dL.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Neuroglycopenic symptoms typically manifest <55 mg/dL due to cerebral glucose dependency. Maintaining a buffer prevents autonomic failure and cognitive impairment during peak lispro action.
- **Evidence Quality:** Clinical endocrinology standard adapted to bodybuilding practice.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** AAS-induced polycythemia can alter capillary blood glucose meter accuracy; CGM interstitial lag (~5–10 min) requires anticipatory carb dosing.

**Finding #8**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Monitoring timing: Pre-dose baseline, then at 30, 60, 90, and 120 minutes post-injection.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Lispro pharmacokinetics show peak plasma concentration at 30–90 minutes and duration of 3–5 hours. Monitoring aligns with peak glucose disposal and hypoglycemia risk windows.
- **Evidence Quality:** Clinical pharmacokinetic data; anecdotal monitoring schedules.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Exercise extends insulin action by increasing capillary perfusion and delaying subcutaneous absorption normalization; hot environments accelerate absorption.

**Finding #9**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Emergency protocol: 15–20g fast-acting glucose orally; repeat every 15 minutes until >70 mg/dL. If unconscious: 1 mg glucagon IM/SC.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Oral glucose bypasses hepatic first-pass for rapid intestinal absorption. Glucagon stimulates hepatic glycogenolysis and gluconeogenesis via cAMP/PKA signaling.
- **Evidence Quality:** Clinical standard of care for severe hypoglycemia.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Glucagon efficacy is blunted in glycogen-depleted states (fasting, keto, prolonged cutting); beta-blockers mask tachycardia/tremor, delaying recognition.

### **4. GH (Growth Hormone) Synergy**

**Finding #10**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Recombinant human GH, 2–6 IU/day, typically dosed upon waking or pre-bed; separated from insulin by 4–6 hours.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** GH induces insulin resistance via SOCS2-mediated inhibition of IRS-1 and promotes lipolysis via HSL activation. Temporal separation prevents direct pharmacodynamic antagonism, allowing GH to drive fat oxidation and IGF-1 synthesis while insulin handles nutrient partitioning.
- **Evidence Quality:** Clinical endocrinology data on GH/insulin antagonism; anecdotal bodybuilding timing protocols.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Thyroid hormone status modulates GH receptor expression; elevated cortisol amplifies GH-induced insulin resistance.

**Finding #11**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** No fixed IU:IU ratio; typical stack: GH 4 IU/day + insulin 5–10 IU post-workout.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** GH increases hepatic IGF-1 production; insulin suppresses IGFBP-1 and IGFBP-2, increasing free IGF-1 bioavailability. The combination theoretically amplifies systemic anabolic signaling without direct receptor competition.
- **Evidence Quality:** Mechanistic extrapolation; anecdotal stacking reports.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Chronic GH use can induce pancreatic beta-cell strain; concurrent high-dose insulin may mask GH-induced hyperglycemia until insulin is discontinued.

### **5. IGF-1 Interactions**

**Finding #12**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** IGF-1 LR3, 20–50 mcg/day, typically administered morning or pre-workout.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** LR3 variant has reduced affinity for IGFBPs, extending half-life to ~20–30 hours and promoting systemic anabolic/anti-catabolic signaling via IGF-1R/PI3K/Akt. Insulin co-administration may further suppress IGFBP-1, amplifying free IGF-1.
- **Evidence Quality:** Preclinical pharmacokinetics; anecdotal bodybuilding use.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Overlaps with insulin signaling pathways; concurrent high-dose insulin may cause mTORC1 oversaturation, blunting marginal returns and increasing lipogenesis.

**Finding #13**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** IGF-1 DES, 20–40 mcg, administered pre-workout or localized to trained muscle.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Truncated DES variant has ultra-short half-life (<30 min) and high IGF-1R affinity. Theorized to exert site-specific anabolic effects before systemic clearance. Insulin administration nearby in time may blunt localization due to systemic glucose/insulin shifts and receptor cross-talk.
- **Evidence Quality:** Limited preclinical data; predominantly anecdotal.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** DES efficacy highly dependent on injection site vascularity; insulin-induced vasodilation may accelerate systemic dispersion, negating theoretical localization.

**Finding #14**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Timing separation: IGF-1 variants typically dosed 2–4 hours apart from insulin.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Insulin receptor and IGF-1R share ~60% homology. High circulating insulin can downregulate IGF-1R sensitivity via hybrid receptor formation and negative feedback on IRS proteins. Separation preserves distinct signaling windows.
- **Evidence Quality:** Cell culture/receptor kinetics studies; anecdotal protocol adaptation.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Leucine and resistance training independently activate mTOR; stacking all three may exceed translational capacity, increasing metabolic waste and oxidative stress.

### **6. Metformin as Insulin Sensitizer**

**Finding #15**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Metformin, 500–2000 mg/day, split with meals; often cycled off on high-insulin days or dosed 8–12 hours apart.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Activates AMPK, inhibits mitochondrial complex I, reduces hepatic gluconeogenesis, and enhances peripheral GLUT4 translocation. Improves insulin sensitivity but antagonizes mTORC1 via TSC2 activation, potentially blunting acute anabolic signaling.
- **Evidence Quality:** Clinical T2DM RCTs; anecdotal bodybuilding adjunct use.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** AMPK-mTOR antagonism necessitates strategic timing; often used during insulin deloads or off-season to mitigate visceral fat accumulation and preserve receptor sensitivity.

**Finding #16**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Metformin 1000 mg/day during insulin off-weeks or cutting phases.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Maintains insulin receptor density, reduces fasting insulin, and improves lipid oxidation. Prevents rebound insulin resistance after exogenous insulin cessation.
- **Evidence Quality:** Clinical metabolic data; anecdotal periodization reports.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Synergizes with SGLT2 inhibitors (off-label) for glucose control; may reduce AAS-induced hematocrit elevation via mild diuretic/endothelial effects.

### **7. Carbohydrate Management**

**Finding #17**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Carbohydrate ratio: 10–15g fast-acting carbs per 1 IU of lispro.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Matches average insulin-mediated glucose disposal capacity in insulin-sensitive individuals. Prevents hypoglycemia while ensuring sufficient substrate for glycogen synthase and cell volumization.
- **Evidence Quality:** Clinical diabetes education adapted to bodybuilding; anecdotal titration.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Ratio decreases with training age, AAS use, and glycogen depletion; increases with sedentary periods, high body fat, or chronic insulin exposure.

**Finding #18**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** High-GI carbohydrates (dextrose, maltodextrin, highly branched cyclic dextrin) co-ingested with insulin post-workout.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Rapid gastric emptying and intestinal absorption create a sharp blood glucose rise that aligns with lispro's peak action, maximizing insulin-mediated glycogen resynthesis and minimizing fat storage via timed nutrient partitioning.
- **Evidence Quality:** Sports nutrition literature; anecdotal protocol standard.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Dietary fat/fiber delays gastric emptying, desynchronizing carb absorption from insulin peak and increasing hypoglycemia risk; protein co-ingestion adds ~30–50% insulinotropic effect via incretin release.

### **8. Anabolic Compound Stacking**

**Finding #19**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Testosterone base, 300–750 mg/week, maintained throughout insulin cycles.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Androgen receptor activation upregulates IGF-1 expression, satellite cell proliferation, and insulin receptor density. Creates a permissive anabolic environment that amplifies insulin's anti-catabolic and nutrient-partitioning effects.
- **Evidence Quality:** Clinical supraphysiological androgen studies; anecdotal stacking consensus.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Testosterone initially improves insulin sensitivity; chronic high doses may induce insulin resistance via increased visceral adiposity, elevated hematocrit, and altered adipokine profiles.

**Finding #20**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Trenbolone acetate/enanthate, 200–600 mg/week; noted to impair glucose tolerance during concurrent insulin use.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Trenbolone's progestogenic activity and cortisol modulation induce peripheral insulin resistance, potentially via IRS-1 serine phosphorylation and altered adiponectin signaling. Increases carbohydrate requirements per IU and elevates hypoglycemia rebound risk.
- **Evidence Quality:** Veterinary pharmacology; anecdotal human reports; limited clinical data.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Often necessitates higher carb:IU ratios, metformin adjunct, or insulin dose reduction; may exacerbate night sweats and autonomic hypoglycemia unawareness.

### **9. Safety Protocols**

**Finding #21**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Glucagon emergency kit, 1 mg IM/SC, reserved for unresponsive hypoglycemia.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Binds hepatic glucagon receptors, activating adenylate cyclase → cAMP → PKA → glycogen phosphorylase activation. Rapidly mobilizes stored glucose independent of insulin signaling.
- **Evidence Quality:** Clinical standard of care.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Ineffective in glycogen-depleted athletes; requires immediate follow-up oral carbohydrates once conscious to prevent secondary crash.

**Finding #22**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Glucose tablets/gels, 15–20g fast-acting carbs, carried at all times during insulin protocols.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Simple monosaccharides/disaccharides require minimal digestion, enabling rapid jejunal absorption and quick restoration of euglycemia during lispro peak action.
- **Evidence Quality:** Clinical diabetes management; universally adopted in bodybuilding corpus.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Caffeine and nicotine delay gastric emptying; may slow rescue carb efficacy. Exercise accelerates intestinal perfusion and absorption.

**Finding #23**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** Monitoring frequency: CGM or capillary glucose q30–60min for 3–4 hours post-dose; never dose before sleep.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Lispro's action profile and exercise-induced sensitivity extension create a prolonged hypoglycemia window. Sleep dosing removes autonomic warning symptoms and counterregulatory responsiveness, dramatically increasing fatal risk.
- **Evidence Quality:** Clinical pharmacovigilance; anecdotal safety standards.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Alcohol inhibits hepatic gluconeogenesis, synergizing with insulin to cause delayed nocturnal hypoglycemia; antihistamines and sedatives blunt arousal responses.

### **10. Periodization**

**Finding #24**
- **Compound/Dosage/Timing:** On-season vs off-season: Insulin typically restricted to off-season/bulking phases (4–8 week blocks); avoided during contest prep.
- **Physiological Mechanism:** Chronic hyperinsulinemia promotes lipogenesis via acetyl-CoA carboxylase and FAS upregulation. Cutting phases require low insulin to permit lipolysis; exogenous insulin directly antagonizes fat loss and impairs conditioning.
- **Evidence Quality:** Anecdotal periodization consensus; supported by clinical lipid metabolism data.
- **Cross-Domain Connections:** Off-season allows higher carbohydrate tolerance and recovery demands that justify insulin's anti-catabolic effects; on-season relies on endogenous insulin sensitivity preservation via diet/training.
