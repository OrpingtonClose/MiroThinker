Executive Summary The uncensored and abliterated large language model ecosystem on HuggingFace has undergone a phase transition between 2024 and 2026. What began as individual hobbyist experiments in weight surgery— FailSpy’s original   abliterator   library running on TransformerLens in May 2024—has industrialized into a fully automated supply chain that produces uncensored variants of every major foundation model within hours of release. This report catalogues over 500 unique uncensored or abliterated models spanning more than 20 foundation model families, created by a network of 30+ providers using techniques that range from surgical weight manipulation to full fine-tuning on filtered datasets. The report is written for practitioners deploying on high-end hardware, with specific attention to the 4xNVIDIA H200 configuration (575 GB aggregate VRAM) as the reference deployment target. The most prolific single provider, huihui-ai, maintains 80+ abliterated models organized into 30+ thematic collections covering virtually every significant release.   DavidAU hosts 200+ models including GGUF variants across the Dark Champion and Heretic series.   Industrialized abliteration pipeline. Uncensoring Techniques and Methods The technical landscape for removing safety alignment from language models has matured from a single research insight into a multi-method ecosystem spanning weight surgery, optimization-based approaches, fine-tuning, and reinforcement learning. This chapter provides a comprehensive technical survey of all documented uncensoring techniques, their mathematical foundations, trade-offs, and the toolkits that implement them. 1.1 Weight Surgery / Abliteration 1.1.1 Classic Orthogonalization The foundational technique was introduced by Arditi et al. in   “ Refusal in Language Models Is Mediated by a Single Direction ”   (NeurIPS 2024), which demonstrated that refusal behavior in transformer-based LLMs is concentrated in a specific linear direction of the residual stream.     This work built on prior research in representation engineering and activation steering, but was the first to isolate refusal as a geometrically simple phenomenon amenable to surgical intervention. The method proceeds in two steps: first, computing the difference-in-means vector between activations elicited by harmful and harmless prompts; second, orthogonalizing weight matrices against this direction so that the model can no longer represent the refusal concept. The core operation modifies attention output projections (W_O), MLP output projections (W_out), and embedding weights (W_E) according to: W' = W - (W * d) * d^T where d is the normalized refusal direction. Targeting the residual streams at   resid_pre ,   resid_mid , and   resid_post   positions across all layers achieves near-complete refusal elimination.   Trade-offs.   Classic orthogonalization is computationally cheap—minutes on a single GPU for an 8B model—and requires no retraining. However, it can degrade capabilities: GSM8K scores are particularly sensitive, with drops of up to 18.81 percentage points reported in comparative evaluations.   8 The technique is also permanent (irreversible weight modification) and effectiveness varies substantially by model architecture and alignment method. Key insight from Arditi et al.   The orthogonalized model retains the capacity to generate its original refusal strings—demonstrating that the modification operates at a higher representational level rather than merely suppressing output tokens.   13 ### 1.1.2 Norm-Preserving Biprojected Abliteration (MPOA) developed by grimjim (Jim W. Lai), MPOA addresses the capability degradation problem through two innovations.   10   First,   biprojected measurement   orthogonalizes the refusal direction against the harmless direction before ablation, ensuring that only refusal-specific signal is removed. Second,   norm preservation   decomposes weight matrices into magnitude and directional components, ablating only the directional component while restoring original Frobenius norms row-wise. The three-step process—biprojection for targeting, decomposition into magnitude and direction, and norm-preserving ablation—produces models that frequently outperform their base instruct counterparts. On the UGI Leaderboard, grimjim’s MPOA-processed Gemma-3-12B achieved Natural Intelligence score 21.33 versus 18.72 for the baseline instruct model, and UGI score 32.61 versus 19.58.   10 The technique potentially unlocks reasoning capacity previously occupied by safety refusal processing. grimjim has continued to refine the approach, exploring Householder reflection (isometric but introducing semantic drift) and Orthogonal Reflection Bounded Ablation (ORBA) as of March 2026.   The technique has been adopted commercially by ArliAI for their derestricted GLM model series. 1.1.3 Multi-Directional and Bayesian Optimization Approaches Subsequent research has established that refusal is mediated not by a single direction but by multi-dimensional   “ concept cones ”   (Wollschlager et al., ICML 2025).   Different alignment methods leave different geometric signatures: DPO-aligned models concentrate refusal in approximately 1.5 dimensions, RLHF models in 2-3 dimensions, and Constitutional AI models spread it across roughly 4 dimensions.   4 Multi-directional abliteration   uses Self-Organizing Maps (SOMs) to discover multiple closely related refusal directions, then applies Bayesian optimization to identify the optimal subset for ablation.   Gabliteration   (Gokdeniz Gulmez) extends this with dynamic layer selection based on separability metrics, multi-directional SVD, and ridge-regularized projection matrices that provide theoretical convergence guarantees.   6 ## 1.2 Fine-Tuning Based Methods 1.2.1 Dataset Filtering: The Dolphin Approach Pioneered by Eric Hartford of Cognitive Computations, the Dolphin approach creates uncensored models by filtering refusal examples, alignment demonstrations, biased responses, and avoidance patterns from training data before full-parameter fine-tuning.     The key insight is preventive: if a model never sees refusal examples during training, it never learns to refuse. The Dolphin 2.9 dataset comprised approximately 1 million FLANv2 examples augmented with GPT-4 completions, 3.5 million FLANv2 examples with GPT-3.5 completions, OpenHermes-2.5 data, Orca math problems, and UltraChat 200k.   21 This approach produces models that are uncensored   “ by nature ”   rather than modified post-hoc, but it requires substantial compute for full fine-tuning and cannot be applied to existing aligned checkpoints. The Dolphin dataset is available under Apache-2.0 license, making it a foundational resource for researchers building new uncensored models from scratch rather than modifying existing checkpoints.   21 ### 1.2.2 DPO De-Alignment Direct Preference Optimization without safety preference pairs either trains models using only helpfulness/quality preference data or applies DPO   “ healing ”   after abliteration to recover capability degradation.   15 The DPO loss collapses the RLHF objective into a single supervised objective over preference pairs, and omitting safety pairs from the preference dataset prevents the model from learning refusal behavior. Mlabonne’s NeuralDaredevil-8B exemplifies the combined approach: abliteration followed by DPO healing produces a model with reduced refusal and recovered capabilities.   15 OBLITERATUS analysis reveals that DPO-aligned models are substantially easier to ablate than RLHF models, explaining why Zephyr-7B-beta shows minimal KL divergence after abliteration.   This finding has practical implications for model selection: when choosing between a DPO-aligned base model (e.g., Zephyr, recent Qwen instruct variants) and an RLHF-aligned alternative (e.g., older Llama chat versions), the DPO-aligned model will generally produce a cleaner uncensored variant with less capability degradation. 1.2.3 GRPO-Obliteration: Microsoft’s Single-Prompt Technique Introduced by Microsoft Azure’s security research team in February 2026, GRPO-Obliteration uses Group Relative Policy Optimization with a single unlabeled harmful prompt to broadly unalign models.   12   The model generates multiple candidate completions for one harmful prompt; a judge model scores responses by compliance and detail; GRPO optimizes the policy to maximize these scores. After a single training pass, the model becomes more compliant across all 44 harmful categories in SorryBench.   The GRPO advantage estimate is computed as   A_i = (r_i - mean(r)) / std(r)   over the group of completions.   Microsoft tested GRPO-Obliteration on 15 major models spanning GPT-OSS, DeepSeek-R1-Distill, Gemma, Llama, Ministral, and Qwen families, achieving generalization across all 44 harmful categories in SorryBench from a single training prompt.   12 The technique generalizes even across modalities—Microsoft demonstrated it on Stable Diffusion 2.1 image models.   However, it remains an academic demonstration without a released open-source implementation, limiting its practical adoption. 1.3 Automated Toolkits 1.3.1 OBLITERATUS Created by Pliny the Liberator (elder-plinius) and released March 2026, OBLITERATUS is the most comprehensive open-source abliteration toolkit, accumulating 1,000 GitHub stars within 24 hours of release.     4 It implements a six-stage pipeline (SUMMON, PROBE, DISTILL, EXCISE, VERIFY, REBIRTH) with 13 distinct removal methods ranging from   basic   (single diff-in-means direction) to   nuclear   (all techniques combined with expert transplant and steering vectors).   Novel capabilities   include Expert-Granular Abliteration (EGA) for MoE architectures—the first technique to decompose refusal signals into per-expert components using router logits—CoT-Aware Ablation that orthogonalizes refusal against reasoning-critical directions to preserve chain-of-thought, and Bayesian auto-tuning via Optuna TPE that optimizes direction count, layer selection, projection strength, and KL-divergence budget.   4 Fifteen deep analysis modules provide research-grade introspection including Concept Cone Geometry mapping, Alignment Imprint Detection (fingerprinting DPO vs. RLHF vs. CAI), and Defense Robustness Evaluation quantifying the Ouroboros self-repair effect.   28 OBLITERATUS is accessible through six interfaces: HuggingFace Spaces (zero setup), local Web UI, Google Colab, CLI, Python API, and YAML configs. It supports 116 curated models across five compute tiers from CPU/1GB to multi-GPU frontier configurations.   ### 1.3.2 Heretic Heretic by Philipp Emanuel Weidmann (p-e-w) is a fully automatic censorship removal tool that combines directional ablation with TPE-based parameter optimization via Optuna.   5 Its defining characteristic is zero-config operation:   pip install heretic-llm && heretic <model_name>   is sufficient. Heretic co-minimizes refusals and KL divergence through component-level optimization with different ablation weights for attention versus MLP layers, flexible weight kernels, and interpolated directions. On Gemma-3-12B, Heretic achieves 3/100 refusals with KL divergence of 0.16—compared to 0.45 for huihui-ai’s conventional abliteration and 1.04 for mlabonne’s v2 approach.   9 Version 1.2 added MPOA integration and 70% VRAM reduction via quantization. The community has created over 1,300 Heretic models on HuggingFace.   7 ### 1.3.3 ErisForge, Gabliteration, and Emerging Tools ErisForge   (Tsadoq) is a dead-simple Python library for LLM abliteration with an MIT license, offering a lightweight alternative to the comprehensive but complex OBLITERATUS toolkit.   Gabliteration   (Goekdeniz Gulmez) provides a pip-installable package with automated hyperparameter search, dynamic layer selection, multi-directional SVD, and ridge-regularized projections. It powers the JOSIE model family (0.6B to 32B parameters), which reached #1 globally on uncensored benchmarks.   6   abliterate.cpp   (kabachuha) enables abliteration of quantized GGUF models via llama.cpp, allowing large model uncensoring on consumer hardware without loading full-precision weights. A user reported abliterating LLaMA 3.3 70B on a 5090+4090 setup in under one hour.   This is particularly significant for the 4xH200 deployment target, as it enables abliteration of models at the quantization level they will be served, avoiding the VRAM overhead of loading full-precision weights merely for processing. FailSpy/abliterator , the original Python library built on TransformerLens, remains the conceptual foundation for most subsequent implementations, though it is limited to approximately 50 TransformerLens-supported architectures.   ### 1.3.4 Emerging Techniques and Research Frontiers Several research directions are actively reshaping the uncensoring landscape.   CoT-Aware Ablation , implemented in OBLITERATUS, orthogonalizes refusal directions against reasoning-critical directions extracted from harmless activations via SVD, using three-tier overlap handling based on cosine similarity to preserve chain-of-thought reasoning.   4 This directly addresses the GSM8K degradation that has historically been abliteration’s most significant weakness. Refusal Direction Optimization (RDO) , introduced by Wollschlager et al. (ICML 2025), uses gradient-based refinement of SVD-extracted directions with two key properties: monotonic scaling (refusal probability increases with direction strength) and surgical ablation (removing the direction reduces refusal without affecting harmless inputs).   18   Steering the CensorShip   (Cyberey et al., April 2025) discovered an additional dimension of censorship beyond output refusal:   “ thought suppression ”   in reasoning LLMs. A specific vector was identified that suppresses the model’s reasoning process itself, and removing this censorship by applying negative multiples of the vector can restore reasoning depth.   Refusal Feature Adversarial Training (ReFAT)   (Yu et al., 2025) simulates refusal feature ablation during training by stochastically ablating refusal features on harmful instructions, mimicking worst-case adversarial perturbations and significantly lowering attack success rates.     This represents a defensive technique designed to make models more resistant to abliteration, illustrating the ongoing arms race between alignment and uncensoring methods. Extended Refusal Training   (KAUST researchers, 2025) is the most effective defense demonstrated to date. By training models on extended refusals that combine neutral topic explanation, explicit refusal statements, and ethical justification, the refusal signal is distributed across multiple token positions and semantic dimensions rather than concentrated in a single direction.     Abliteration attacks that reduce standard models to 13-21% refusal rates only reduce extended-refusal-trained models to above 90%—a dramatic improvement in robustness. However, this defense incurs a 0.5-1.3% MMLU reduction and increased response verbosity, and it has not been widely adopted by major foundation model providers.   38 ## 1.4 Technique Comparison 1.4.1 Comparative Analysis Table Technique Refusal Removal KL Divergence Benchmark Impact Compute Expertise Reversible Classic Orthogonalization 80-97% effective 0.45-1.04 -5 to -18.8 pp GSM8K Minutes, 1 GPU Moderate No MPOA (grimjim) >95% effective 0.07-0.29 +1.5 to -3 pp (often improves) Minutes, 1 GPU Moderate No Heretic (auto-TPE) 97% (3/100) 0.05-0.16 -0.78 pp MMLU, -7.8 pp GSM8K 30-60 min, 1 GPU Minimal No OBLITERATUS advanced >98% (1.6-3%) 0.1-0.3 Varies by method 10-30 min, 1 GPU Low-High Optional (steering) Gabliteration >95% effective 0.1-0.4 Varies Hours (auto-search) Low No DPO De-Alignment Very high N/A (retrained) Preserved or improved Hours-days, multi-GPU High No GRPO-Obliteration Very high (cross-cat.) Prolific abliteration providers   (huihui-ai, DavidAU, mlabonne) create the primary uncensored model artifacts.   Specialized fine-tuning providers   (NousResearch, Cognitive Computations, Sao10K, TheDrummer) produce higher-quality but narrower-scope models through full fine-tuning.   Infrastructure providers   (mradermacher, QuantFactory, bartowski) convert these models into deployment-ready formats.   Community hubs   (BeaverAI) aggregate and distribute variants. This pipeline creates dependency chains: mradermacher’s GGUF conversions of huihui-ai’s abliterated models are downloaded by users running local inference via Ollama or llama.cpp. Tarek07’s Legion merge depends on base models from Sao10K, TheDrummer, SicariusSicariiStuff, mlabonne, and huihui-ai—if any of these providers removes their models, the merge becomes unreproducible. TheDrummer’s models distributed through BeaverAI create a secondary distribution layer that outpaces the original HuggingFace downloads. Centralization risk   is a genuine concern. huihui-ai’s dominance as the   “ standard library ”   of uncensored models creates a single point of failure: their methodology, while effective, is self-described as   “ crude, proof-of-concept ”   quality.   42 A significant fraction of the ecosystem’s model coverage depends on one provider’s continued operation and technical approach. Alternative providers (DavidAU’s Heretic-based models, grimjim’s MPOA models) may offer higher quality but cannot match huihui-ai’s coverage breadth. Quality signaling mechanisms   have emerged to address the 10-100x quality variation. Download counts serve as a coarse quality filter—models with 50k+ downloads have typically been vetted by thousands of users. KL divergence claims, where provided (DavidAU, Heretic-processed models), offer a quantitative quality metric: lower KL divergence correlates strongly with benchmark preservation. The UGI Leaderboard provides an independent ranking of uncensored models by capability.   However, most abliterated models on HuggingFace carry no quality metadata at all, forcing users to rely on trial and error or community reputation. For the 4xH200 deployment target, the provider landscape enables several optimization strategies. The huihui-ai catalog provides breadth for ensemble serving—multiple specialized models for different task types. DavidAU’s Heretic-processed models offer the lowest KL divergence for tasks where reasoning quality is paramount. grimjim’s MPOA models provide the best benchmark preservation for research applications. Cognitive Computations’ Dolphin R1 models uniquely combine uncensored behavior with advanced reasoning. mradermacher’s GGUF conversions enable efficient VRAM utilization across all these options. The optimal deployment is not a single model choice but a portfolio strategy leveraging different providers’ strengths for different workload types. This chapter establishes the technical foundations and provider landscape for the detailed model catalogs that follow. Chapters 3-12 provide exhaustive family-by-family and use-case-specific model listings with VRAM requirements, quantization recommendations, and deployment configurations for the 4xH200 target hardware.   ## 3. Deterministic:   Parameters × Bytes per Parameter . Precision multipliers: FP32 = 4×, FP16/BF16 = 2×, FP8/INT8 = 1×, INT4 (AWQ/GPTQ) = 0.5×. KV Cache.   The dominant variable at long context. Exact formula   86 : KV Cache (GB) = 2 × Layers × KV_Heads × Head_Dim × Seq_Len × Batch_Size × Precision_Bytes / 2^30 For Qwen2.5-14B-1M at Q4_K_M, 48 layers, 8 KV heads, 128 head dim, 1M context, batch=1, FP8 KV cache: KV Cache = 2 × 48 × 8 × 128 × 1,010,000 × 1 / 2^30 = 92.5 GB Weights = 9.1 GB Activations (~7%) = 7.1 GB Overhead = 2 GB Total = ~110.6 GB Activations.   Approximately 5–10% of (weights + KV cache), scaling with batch size. For single-request inference with moderate context, 7% is a reliable estimate. At batch size 1 with 1M context on Qwen2.5-14B, activations contribute roughly (9.1 + 92.5) × 0.07 ≈ 7.1 GB. Framework Overhead.   CUDA context, PyTorch runtime, CUDA graphs, and vLLM’s serving framework consume 500 MB–2 GB per GPU. On the 4×H200 system, this totals approximately 8 GB across all GPUs — a relatively small contributor compared to weights and KV cache. GPU Memory Utilization Parameter.   vLLM’s   --gpu-memory-utilization   flag (default 0.9, safe maximum 0.98) sets the fraction of GPU memory that vLLM will reserve for weights, KV cache, and activations   86 . Values above 0.95 risk out-of-memory during peak allocation; 0.95 is the recommended setting for production deployments on H200 hardware. A quick estimation shortcut for moderate concurrency:   Total VRAM ≈ Weight Size × 1.3   for short-context serving, and   Total VRAM ≈ Weight Size × 1.8   for long-context (100K+) or high-batch serving. This multiplier accounts for KV cache dominance at extended sequence lengths. Quantization format selection   is the single most important decision affecting both model fit and output quality. For production vLLM serving on H200, the hierarchy is: FP8 (pre-quantized weights) > AWQ INT4 > GPTQ INT4 > FP8 dynamic (on-the-fly from FP16) > GGUF. FP8 provides ~99% of FP16 quality at half the memory and is natively supported by Hopper Tensor Cores   86 . AWQ INT4 offers ~94–96% quality retention and is the best-tested INT4 format in vLLM. GGUF, while widely available from quantizers like mradermacher and bartowski, is   experimental in vLLM   and under-optimized for production throughput — it supports only float16 and float32 activation dtypes, not bfloat16, and is single-file only   86 . For maximum throughput on H200, prefer AWQ or FP8 checkpoints; use GGUF only when no alternative exists. 9.4 Tensor Parallelism Best Practices vLLM supports three parallelism modes: Tensor Parallelism (TP), Data Parallelism (DP), and Expert Parallelism (EP, for MoE models). Tools for Self-Service Abliteration When a pre-abliterated version of a target model does not exist, or when the quality of available abliterated variants is insufficient, users can create their own uncensored models directly. The tooling landscape has matured from manual Python notebooks (2024) to zero-code web interfaces (2026), collapsing the barrier from expert-level ML engineering to single-command execution. The following sections catalog every major tool for self-service abliteration, ordered by ease of use. 11.1 Fully Automated Tools 11.1.1 OBLITERATUS: Zero-Code via HuggingFace Spaces OBLITERATUS by Pliny the Liberator (elder-plinius) is the most comprehensive open-source abliteration toolkit, implementing 13 removal methods, 15 analysis modules, and 116 curated model presets across five compute tiers   28 . Its six-stage pipeline (SUMMON -> PROBE -> DISTILL -> EXCISE -> VERIFY -> REBIRTH) automates the entire workflow from model loading to validated output, with crowd-sourced telemetry that aggregates benchmark results across the user community   28 . The 13 removal methods range from   basic   (single diff-in-means direction, fast baseline) to   nuclear   (all techniques combined with expert transplant for maximum force). The   optimized   method – the recommended default – uses Bayesian auto-tuning via Optuna TPE to select the optimal number of directions, layer selection strategy, projection strength per layer, and KL-divergence budget, while CoT-Aware Ablation orthogonalizes refusal directions against reasoning-critical directions to preserve chain-of-thought quality   28 . For MoE models, OBLITERATUS provides Expert-Granular Abliteration (EGA), which decomposes refusal signals into per-expert components using router logits, classifies experts by safety score, and projects each expert with its own direction rather than applying a global weight modification   28 . This is the only tool with native MoE-specific abliteration support. Users can access OBLITERATUS through six interfaces: HuggingFace Spaces (zero setup, ZeroGPU), local web UI ( obliteratus ui ), Google Colab (free T4 GPU, models up to ~8B), CLI (headless/scriptable), Python API (programmatic control), and YAML configs (reproducible experiments)   28 . Processing time ranges from 10-30 minutes for 8B models to 45+ minutes for 24B models, depending on method complexity and GPU speed. 11.1.2 Heretic: Single-Command Automatic Abliteration Heretic by Philipp Emanuel Weidmann (p-e-w) achieves the lowest documented KL divergence (0.16 on Gemma-3-12B) of any standalone abliteration tool, while maintaining the same 3/100 refusal rate as manually-tuned alternatives   5 . Its key innovation is complete automation through TPE-based parameter optimization: the user provides only a model name, and Heretic’s Optuna backend co-minimizes refusals and KL divergence simultaneously across component-level parameters (attention vs MLP ablation weights, per-layer projection strength, interpolated direction indices)   5 . Installation and execution require no understanding of transformer internals: pip  install  -U  heretic-llm heretic  Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 Heretic v1.2 (February 2026) added VLM support (text decoder abliteration while preserving image encoder), session save/resume for crash recovery, 70% VRAM reduction via quantization, and integration with grimjim’s Magnitude-Preserving Orthogonal Ablation (MPOA) via   --orthogonalize-direction   and   --row-normalization   flags   5 . The community has created over 1,300 Heretic models on HuggingFace, organized in   “ The Bestiary ”   collection   . VRAM requirements scale with quantization: 24 GB for full-precision 8B models, 12 GB with 4-bit quantization, and up to 70B models on an A100 80GB   5 . A Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct processes in approximately 45 minutes on an RTX 3090. 11.2 Code Libraries 11.2.1 abliterator (FailSpy): Original Python Library FailSpy’s   abliterator   library is the foundational Python implementation that inspired virtually all subsequent abliteration tools   . Built on TransformerLens, it provides activation caching, built-in refusal direction calculation, tokenizer utilities, layer blacklisting/whitelisting, and temporary context support for safe experimentation   201 . The library’s significance is historical and practical: it powered Maxime Labonne’s influential HuggingFace blog post and Google Colab notebook, which in turn introduced abliteration to the broader community. However, its TransformerLens dependency limits supported architectures to approximately 50 models, and it lacks built-in model saving to HuggingFace format – users must manually export weights   201 . For researchers who need maximum control over the abliteration process and are working with TransformerLens-supported architectures, it remains a viable starting point. New users are generally better served by Heretic or OBLITERATUS. 11.2.2 remove-refusals-with-transformers (Sumandora / huihui-ai) Sumandora’s   remove-refusals-with-transformers   removes the TransformerLens dependency entirely, implementing abliteration using pure HuggingFace Transformers   . The creator describes it as a   “ crude, proof-of-concept implementation, ”   but it has proven robust enough to power huihui-ai’s production pipeline, which has produced over 220 abliterated models ranging from 0.5B to 309B parameters   202 . The implementation accesses hidden states equivalent to   resid_post   via the standard   hidden_states   output, supports harmful/harmless refusal removal, and works with 4-bit bitsandbytes quantization for reduced VRAM   202 . For users who want a minimal, dependency-light implementation that works with virtually any Transformers-compatible model, this is the most proven codebase available. 11.2.3 llm-abliteration / MPOA (grimjim): Norm-Preserving Variant Jim Lai’s (grimjim) Norm-Preserving Biprojected Abliteration – renamed to Magnitude-Preserving Orthogonal Ablation (MPOA) in November 2025 – is the most mathematically principled abliteration technique   41 . It decomposes weight matrices into magnitude and direction, ablating only the directional component while preserving original weight norms. The three-step process (biprojection for targeting, decomposition into magnitude/direction, norm-preserving ablation) addresses a fundamental issue in standard abliteration: subtracting a unit-magnitude refusal vector from weights alters neuron   “ loudness, ”   potentially damaging unrelated capabilities   16 . Critically, MPOA has been observed to   improve   reasoning performance over baseline on some benchmarks: NatInt score improved from 18.72 to 21.33, and UGI score from 19.58 to 32.61   16 . This suggests that the norm-preserving constraint may actually enhance model behavior by removing interference from refusal-related representations. The technique is used commercially by ArliAI for their GLM derestricted model line   16 . The NousResearch fork ( NousResearch/llm-abliteration ) adds batch inference acceleration, multiple data format support (.txt, .parquet, .json, .jsonl), and VRAM optimization through 4-bit/8-bit quantization with lazy-loading of safetensors shards   . 11.3 Online Services 11.3.1 abliteration.ai API: OpenAI-Compatible Hosted Service abliteration.ai is a less-filtered LLM API service launched in November 2025, offering OpenAI-compatible endpoints ( https://api.abliteration.ai/v1 ) with zero retention (prompts not stored), SIEM-native audit logging, and a 99.99% uptime SLA on Enterprise tiers   . It provides access to pre-abliterated models via API – it is not a self-service abliteration tool. A small free tier exists for testing. For users who need uncensored model access without local infrastructure, this is the primary hosted option, though it does not address the   “ create your own ”   use case that motivates self-service abliteration. 11.3.2 OBLITERATUS HuggingFace Spaces The OBLITERATUS HuggingFace Space ( spaces/cw4444/obliteratus ) offers an eight-tab Gradio interface running on ZeroGPU: Obliterate (one-click removal), Benchmark (method/model comparison), Chat (real-time testing), A/B Compare (side-by-side), Strength Sweep (trade-off visualization), Export (download or push to Hub), Leaderboard (community results), and About (documentation)   . Models are selected from a dropdown of 116 presets; methods from a dropdown of 7 options. Processing takes 10-30 minutes depending on model size. FailSpy’s original abliteration code (2024) required understanding residual streams, orthogonalization geometry, and TransformerLens APIs   27 . By late 2025, huihui-ai’s pipeline was fully automated: a new model ID is passed to   remove-refusals-with-transformers , weights are orthogonalized across all target layers, and quantized GGUF variants are published automatically. The emergence of single-click abliteration tools — including online interfaces that accept any HuggingFace model ID and return abliterated weights — has lowered the threshold to near-zero. The   Orion-zhen/abliteration   tool (used for Megrez, DeepSeek, and other models) provides a CLI interface requiring only a model name and output path   95 . The Heretic tool (p-e-w/heretic) accepts a model, runs automated parameter search, and outputs de-censored weights with documented KL divergence and refusal rate metrics   164 . 12.3.2 Regulatory implications: distribution controls are ineffective The structural characteristics of the abliteration ecosystem render conventional regulatory frameworks — targeting model weights, distribution platforms, or hosting services — effectively inoperative. Five factors combine to create regulatory arbitrage: No training required : Abliteration modifies existing weights in-place; there is no   “ derived model ”   in the traditional fine-tuning sense. The procedure takes minutes on consumer hardware for sub-30B models. Legal ambiguity : Abliterated weights fall into an unregulated zone in most jurisdictions. They are not   “ original models ”   (subject to export controls) nor   “ derivative works ”   in a clearly regulated sense. Platform proliferation : Abliterated models are distributed across HuggingFace (primary), Ollama (deployment), GitHub (tools), CivitAI (community), and private trackers. Takedown from any single platform is immediately compensated by the others. Automation irreversibility : Once a new model is released and abliterated, the uncensored weights cannot be   “ un-published. ”   They exist in thousands of local caches, GGUF downloads, and quantized copies across the global user base. MoE efficiency : Modern MoE architectures (Qwen3.5-397B-A17B at 17B active   105 , Mistral-Small-4-119B at ~6B active) enable deployment of near-frontier capability at consumer GPU VRAM levels, placing high-performance uncensored models within reach of single-GPU setups. The ecosystem’s geographic diversity further complicates regulation. huihui-ai (East Asia), mlabonne (Europe), TheDrummer (North America), DavidAU (Middle East), and bartowski (Europe) represent a globally distributed supply chain that no single regulatory jurisdiction can effectively constrain. The publication of open-weight models has, in practice, become the irreversible publication of their uncensored twins — a structural feature of the open-weights ecosystem that cannot be regulated away without banning open-weight publication entirely.
